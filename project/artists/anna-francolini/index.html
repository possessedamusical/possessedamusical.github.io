Anna Francolini

Anna trained at Warwick University and NYT. Her work in theatre includes: Caroline or Change, Metropolis Kabarett and Almost Like Being in Love for the National; Merrily We Roll Along and Company at the Donmar Warehouse; Lee Miller in Six Pictures of Lee Miller at the Minerva, Chichester; Little Jo, in The Ballad of Little Jo, Floyd Collins and Saturday Night at the Bridewell; Things You Shouldn’t Say Past Midnight at the Soho; Spittin’ Distance at the Stephen Joseph Theatre, Scarborough; Anatol at the Arcola; Romeo and Juliet with English Touring Theatre; The Tempest with A & BC; Daisy Pulls It Off at the Lyric; Mahler’s Conversation at the Aldwych; and A Midsummer Night’s Dream with Oxford Stage Company; the West End production of Onassis; Taking Steps (The Orange Tree); The Prime of Miss Jean Brodie (Northampton Royal & Edinburgh, TMA Award Best Performance in a Play); Awaking Beauty (Stephen Joseph Theatre); Three Sisters on Hope Street (Liverpool Everyman and Hampstead); Alphabetical Order (Salisbury Playhouse); Into the Woods (Royal Opera House).

Her work on TV: Live Girls, Rome, Holby City, Lie With Me, Down to Earth, This is Dom Joly, Jonathan Creek, Company and Mash and Peas Do U.S.

Films include: Topsy Turvy, The Final Curtain, The Barn and Z.
